Logarithms

The exponent that a specific base must be raised to in order to get a certain value.
log2 8 = 3
log3 9 = 2
log10 10000 = 4

Product Property: The logarithm of a product is equal to the sum of the logarithms of its factors
log2 (4*2) = log2 4 + log2 2
log3 (6*5) = log3 6 + log3 5
log (100*5) = log 100 + log 5

Quotient Property: The logarithm of a quotient is the logarithm of the divident minus the logarithm of the divisor
log2 (64/8) = log2 64 - log2 8
log6 (36/6) = log6 36 - log6 6
log12 (25/5) = log12 25 - log12 5

Power Property: The logarithm of a power is the product of the exponent and the logarithm of the base
log32 2864 = 64 * log32 28
log2 43 = 3 * log2 4
log94 2347 = 47 * log94 4

Inverse Property: If the logarithm's base is the same as the number itself, then they cancel out and drop the exponent down
log2 24 = 4
log5 57 = 7
log12 1234 = 34

Change of Base Formula: Formula that converts a logarithm to base 10 or base e
log2 7 = (log10 7)/(log10 2)
log5 8 = (log10 8)/(log10 5)
log6 9 = (log10 9)/(log10 6)
